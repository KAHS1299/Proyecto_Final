CRISP-ML Methodology

From tourism problem to monitored AI service

The platform is organized around six CRISP-ML phases: business understanding, data engineering, model engineering, evaluation, deployment, and monitoring.

01

Business Understanding

Tourism overcrowding affects mobility, hotel availability, public services, resident quality of life, and visitor satisfaction in heritage towns.

Goal: predict Low, Medium, or High saturation. Impact: smarter routes, booking decisions, and public planning.
02

Data Engineering

The dataset combines town, season, holiday flag, weather, mobility, events, month, historical tourists, occupancy, and estimated tourist demand.

Raw dataCleaningFeature engineeringModel-ready table
03

Model Engineering

Random Forest was selected because it handles non-linear demand patterns, categorical variables, and feature importance interpretation.

Input FeaturesPreprocessorRandom ForestSaturation + Confidence
04

Model Evaluation

Accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, confusion matrix, and feature importance are exposed in the AI Analytics module.

05

Deployment

Flask serves the user interface, prediction API, dashboard API, map API, and monitoring API. Bootstrap, Chart.js, Leaflet, and AOS power the presentation layer.

06

Monitoring

The platform simulates live drift, confidence degradation, and retraining indicators to explain how AI systems are maintained after deployment.
